Pre-Planning

Pre-planning and spending the time on process. A broken process can break relationships and affect how decisions are made. Spend enough time pre-planning to learn how people contribute. Teams will be more successful if process & relationships are prioritized.

Stakeholder Analysis

A stakeholder is who's making the decision. “Who else might care”, “who else might be impacted”, “who might be affected”. Decision process, sometimes not given enough clarity. Sometimes not all are identified, “Is this group accountable for the final decision or making a recommendation”, they might be making a recommendation that might be over-ruled. Stakeholder, customer, is impacted by, the people that need to implement, then boss, people that could get in the way. Identify the people that have objections, do ladder of influence to find the reason for objection, then include later.

"This group will create a series of concept in which we will test to see which ones move forward". A customer is a stakeholder. Brainstorming Collaboratively

Open

  • Affinity diagram: "Thank you all for coming today” “we’re here today to talk about_” “every idea is a good idea” “causes not solutions”, “get in groups”, “talk about as many ideas as possible” “at 8 minutes” (brainstorming). Restate objective. (Measuring the success of the meeting).

Narrow/Cluster

  • Establish criteria before narrow. “Establishing criteria for choosing items to brainstorm” “I’m going to make a process proposal” “Yes, establish criteria” “Does everyone all understand what were doing” (check for clarification) “Remember were trying to gain consensus”
  • Thank you for participating. (Flexible, allow people to feel safe). Validating, thank you. (Showing sensitivity, find a better word other than neutral). Get someone to help you arrange, back too much to people.

Close

  • Restate criteria for developing solutions: "Thank you everyone, to recap, in our last session..." "The outcome for today is to decide on 2 or 3 solutions." “Just as a reminder, 2-3 causes”. “Throw it out to the group”. “Does anyone have any ideas on the top problem”. “Do you want to expand?”.
  • Tool: Check for agreement, negative poll: "Heather has suggested _ as the top problem, does anyone disagree?"
  • Could skip voting, “Is there one solution people want to move forward with?” “Is this something that can be taken offline?” for off topic suggestions

Listening & Laddering

How to become more conscious to behaviors, seek to understand, work it out together.

  • Bracket (radio channel), inner dialog: park it and put it aside, stay present with person, dialog happening in your head before, bracket, put it is aside, stay present rather than try to think about what they might ask, noticing it, stay curious, appreciative inquiry, recognize your feelings and move on, meditation
  • Reflect: demonstrate someone was heard, repeat what they said. Paraphrase, summarize in own words what I heard
  • Perception check: what hasn’t been said, emotions, my perception is that you don’t feel ready for the launch, inferring correctly, body language versus explicit statement, noticing tension, allows people to open up
  • Open ended question: what, why, how, who - exploring territory, people won’t be honest, judgement embedded, to get more info, not to validate thinking, how to filter questions, say what you observe, “I noticed you have your arms folded...”, true open ended question, void of a point of view, be clear about the bias we have,
  • Ladder of inference (Jumping to conclusions)
  • Not even conscious of it, what you’re paying attention to, from prehistoric times for survival, affects our ability to listen.
  • Be aware of your bias, if others are doing it, as open ended questions, if there’s a data point didn’t consider,
  • “Tell me more about...” “I’m noticing a response, is there something there?”

Resolving Conflict & Differences

Resolving Conflict, Interventions & Reconciling differences Resolving Conflict (4-7) - Going to compromise is not always the way to resolve conflict - “Interests level” below what’s obvious, both could have got what wanted - “Position level” compromise - Key to resolving conflict Interventions When things don’t go as planned. boomerang: throw it out the team, help people move forward, be in tune with the room, deal with it or not, if deal with it, how to let them feel heard, use the group to make a decision, is this something we want to spend time on, Regain focus, Ask/say what’s going on, Body language Void: encourage group discussion, void, take away hierarchy, allow people to be independent, brainstorm. Form: want something from you, if there are sidebars going on in room, conflict. Balance these two, natural thing to do: void, “we have a conflict, that’s okay” “I see you saying X, what does the rest of the group think?”, understand your discomfort areas, move into them. Dominating personalities: enforce process agreements, combine with body language: accept, legitimize/deal with or defer, they would love it if engaged, acknowledge and move away, don’t nod and nod and nod. “group, do we want to defer what were talking about today or put this in the parking lot”. " just want to remind everyone we really want to reach consensus, we want to move the initiative forward and get it out during the meeting," "people changing minds after meeting slows us down, is there something we could be doing different in the meeting so we don’t need to revisit this later, does anyone have any ideas?" “Do you two have anything you’d like to share” “I noticed you have an interesting conversation today” Reconciling differences - Why rephrase, clarification: to find a clear understanding, to make them fell heard. Bracket, open ended questions: hear the other position, be curious about other’s position, ! Focus on agreement rather than disagreement, Guidelines for reconciling differences

  1. Restate each party’s position and empathize with their respective situations
  2. Discover each party’s underlying interests
  3. Propose options without asking for commitment
  4. Build small agreements
Example: Shared interest: taking care of loved ones
